FAQs: Carbon Footprint Calculators

Are free carbon footprint calculators accurate?

Yes, quality free calculators use scientifically-backed emission factors. CarbonChecks uses EPA and IPCC data for accurate calculations. However, accuracy depends on how detailed your inputs are.

How often should I calculate my carbon footprint?

Calculate annually to track progress. Recalculate after major lifestyle changes (moving, new car, home improvements) to see the impact of your decisions.

What's a good carbon footprint per person?

The global average is about 4.8 tons CO₂/year per person. To meet climate goals, we need to reduce to 2 tons by 2050. The US average is 16 tons - significantly above target.

Can I offset my carbon footprint?

Yes, but reduction is better than offsetting. Focus on reducing your footprint first, then offset what you can't eliminate through verified carbon offset programs.

Which calculator is best for beginners?

CarbonChecks is ideal for beginners because it includes an electricity estimator (you don't need to know your exact kWh), provides clear explanations, and offers actionable product recommendations.
